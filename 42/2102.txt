  (a) T4Prohibition on Disclosing Procurement Information.–
    (1) T4In generalK.–Except as provided by law, a person described in paragraph (3) shall not knowingly disclose contractor bid or proposal information or source selection information before the award of a Federal agency procurement contract to which the information relates.
    (2) T4Employee of private sector organizationK.–In addition to the restriction in paragraph (1), an employee of a private sector organization assigned to an agency under chapter 37 of title 5 shall not knowingly disclose contractor bid or proposal information or source selection information during the 3-year period after the employee“s assignment ends, except as provided by law.
    (3) T4ApplicationK.–Paragraph (1) applies to a person that–
      (A)        (i) is a present or former official of the Federal Government; or
        (ii) is acting or has acted for or on behalf of, or who is advising or has advised the Federal Government with respect to, a Federal agency procurement; and
      (B) by virtue of that office, employment, or relationship has or had access to contractor bid or proposal information or source selection information.
  (b) T4Prohibition on Obtaining Procurement InformationK.–Except as provided by law, a person shall not knowingly obtain contractor bid or proposal information or source selection information before the award of a Federal agency procurement contract to which the information relates.
