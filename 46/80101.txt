  (a) T4Duties of Consular OfficerK.–When a vessel of the United States is stranded on a coast of a foreign country, the consular officer in that country shall take proper measures, to the extent the laws of that country allow, to–
    (1) save and secure the vessel and property on the vessel; and
    (2) prepare an inventory of the property that is saved.
  (b) T4Delivery to OwnerK.–After deducting the expenses, the consular officer shall deliver the property, with an inventory, to the owner of the property.
  (c) T4Limitation on Taking PossessionK.–A consular officer may not take possession of property under this section when the owner, master, or consignee is present or able to take possession of the property.
