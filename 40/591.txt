  (a) T4General Limitation on Use of AmountsK.–A department, agency, or instrumentality of the Federal Government may not use amounts appropriated or made available by any law to purchase electricity in a manner inconsistent with state law governing the provision of electric utility service, including–
    (1) state utility commission rulings; and
    (2) electric utility franchises or service territories established under state statute, state regulation, or state-approved territorial agreements.
  (b) T4Exceptions.–
    (1) T4Energy savingsK.–This section does not preclude the head of a federal agency from entering into a contract under section 801 of the National Energy Conservation Policy Act (42 U.S.C. 8287).
    (2) T4Energy savings for military installationsK.–This section does not preclude the Secretary of a military department from–
      (A) entering into a contract under section 2394 \1\N of title 10; or
      (B) purchasing electricity from any provider if the Secretary finds that the utility having the applicable state-approved franchise (or other service authorization) is unwilling or unable to meet unusual standards of service reliability that are necessary for purposes of national defense.
