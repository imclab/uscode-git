  (a) The Chief of the Bureau of Medicine and Surgery shall be appointed by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, for a term of four years, from officers on the active-duty list of the Navy in the Medical Corps. He has the title of Surgeon General. The Surgeon General, while so serving has the grade of vice admiral.
  (b) An officer on the active-duty list of the Navy in the Medical Corps may be detailed as Deputy Chief of the Bureau of Medicine and Surgery.
